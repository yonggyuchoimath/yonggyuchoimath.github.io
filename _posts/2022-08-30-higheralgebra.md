<p>
    The only speaker of this seminar is Yong-Gyu Choi. Incomplete notes are available <a href="https://www.dropbox.com/s/h1qco3ff8w3rt02/book_partial.pdf?dl=0">here</a>.
</p>
<p>
    List of talks:
</p>
<ol>
    <li>
        <p>
            Motivating example (Aug. 30th) 
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: This talk is the very first talk for a semester long series on higher algebra. Higher algebra is the study of algebraic objects in spaces, correcting abnormal behavior of classical homological algebra and incorporating spheres into algebra. In this talk, we will see a concrete example of a higher-algebraic structure on singular cochains, and what structures are needed to formalize such structures with concrete diagrams. We assume familiarity with algebraic topology, category theory and homological algebra (at the first year graduate level).
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            Infinity-categories (Sep. 6th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We define the notion of infinity-categories and Kan complex using observations from the previous talk. A process, called the nerve construction, producing infinity-categories from usual categories will be introduced and we will set dictionaries between them. Infinity-categories of functors will be introduced as well.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            Homotopy coherent nerves (Sep. 13th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We introduce the homotopy coherent nerve of Kan-enriched categories, and show they give rise to infinity-categories. We also discuss homotopy theory of Kan complexes.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            Composition in infinity-categories (Sep. 20th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We define the notion of homotopies and compositions in infinity-categories, and discuss Joyal's theorem characterizing isomorphisms. We give a way to parametrize all possible compositions for given morphisms. For this, we introduce the notion of left and right lifting properties and employ them to study trivial Kan fibrations. We apply a similar argument to define left/right/Kan fibrations.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            Fibrations of infinity-categories (Oct. 4th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We apply various properties of fibrations of infinity-categories to recover notions in classical category theory. We also introduce the notion of localizations of simplicial sets.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            Cocartesian fibrations (Oct. 11th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We introduce the notion of cocartesian fibrations of simplicial sets, and check several concrete examples. Lurie's classification theorem of cocartesian fibrations, which is the central result of the first half of the series, will be presented without proof. To see how it is applied in concrete way, we introduce limits and colimits in infinity-categories and introduce terminologies regarding them.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            Limits (Oct. 25th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We describe limits and colimits inside the infinity-category of small infinity-categories, using classification of cocartesian fibrations. We define commutative monoid objects and, in particular, symmetric monoidal infinity-categories. We explicitly see how much data is stored in such notions. Lastly, we introduce the notion of Kan extensions and discuss its existence and uniqueness up to contractibility.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            Yoneda lemma and Stable infinity-categories (Nov. 1st)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We construct the cartesian symmetric monoidal infinity-category and discuss various phenomena regarding Yoneda embeddings, using the trivial Kan fibrations obtained from left Kan extensions. We now start discussion on higher algebra, starting from the definition of stable infinity-categories.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            More on Stable infinity-categories (Nov. 8th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We discuss basic properties of stable infinity-categories, including t-structure. Using the Dold--Kan correspondence, the derived infinity-category of (nice) abelian category is defined and its universal property is described. Moreover, by recalling the Brown representability theorem, we define the infinity-category of spectra which parametrized generalized cohomology theories on (pointed) topological spaces.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>
            The infinity-category of spectra (Nov. 15th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We give a purely infinity-categorical construction of Eilenberg--MacLane spaces and spectra, and see how these define singular homology and cohomology. We give another equivalent definition of the infinity-category of spectra, and the Moore spectra are introduced. Lastly, the notion of infinity-operad is defined.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>Smash product of spectra (Nov. 22nd)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We discuss what information is encoded in a map of infinity-operads using only its definition. We give a definition of Day convolution on functor infinity-categories, and apply it to produce the smash product of pointed Kan complexes and the smash product of spectra.
        </p>
    </li>

    <li>
        <p>Ring spectra (Nov. 29th)
        </p>
        <p>
            Abstract: We define the notion of ring spectra.
        </p>
    </li>
</ol>
